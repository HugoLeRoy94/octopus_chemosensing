Figure A — Mutual information versus receptor count.
Each point is the mean over 90 randomly generated environments; shaded bands span the 10th–90th percentile. The black dashed line marks the perfect array, H(A) = R (1 bit per receptor), the information-theoretic upper bound for R binary receptors. Homomers track this bound closely. Because a homomeric array of n_g genes is limited to n_g receptors, extending R beyond the gene count requires heteromerization: at fixed n_g, heteromer arrays gain mutual information past the homomer ceiling. Within the heteromeric arrays, cascading construction (homomers first, then dimers, trimers, …) outperforms uniform random stoichiometry sampling. The narrow inter-environment spread shows that, in this regime, the result is insensitive to the detailed environmental parameters.
Figure B — Mutual information decomposed into ligand identity and concentration.
Mutual information versus number of receptors R, separating the array's ability to encode ligand family identity (left, I(A;f)) from ligand concentration (right, I(A;c)). Curves compare homomeric arrays (solid) to heteromeric arrays (dashed). For family identity, all arrays saturate the log(5) ceiling set by the five ligand families, leaving no room for a heteromerization advantage. For concentration, heteromeric arrays exceed homomers, indicating that the benefit of heteromerization is concentrated in concentration coding.
Figure C — Gene expression conditioned on the number of co-expressed genes.
Top: distribution of the number of distinct CR genes expressed per cell. Bottom: per-gene enrichment, defined as P(gene expressed | cell expresses k genes) / (k/N), where N is the total number of genes; white marks the flat expectation (enrichment = 1), at which expression is insensitive to k. Two gene classes emerge: loners, enriched in cells expressing few genes, and joiners, enriched only once other genes are already expressed. This loner/joiner asymmetry provides a mechanism for the non-uniform distribution of genes per cell shown in the top panel, consistent with an optimal heteromerization strategy.
