# Notations
- $k_\text{sub}$: number of sub-units in a receptor (typically 5)

- $n_\text{genes}$ (or $n_\text{units}$): number of possible proteins/genes (typically 26)

- $\mathcal{U} = (u_1,u_2,\cdots u_{k_\text{sub}}) \in \mathbb{R}^{k_\text{sub}}\times \mathbb{R}^{n_\text{unit}}$: identity of a detector (homo-pentamers looks like (1,1,1,1,1)) where $u_i \in \mathbb{R}^{n_\text{unit}}$

- $r \in \mathbb{R}^{k_\text{sub}\times n_\text{unit}}$: index / identity of a receptor. Compation of the $\mathcal{U}$

- $\ell \in \mathbb{N}^{L}$: index / identity of a ligand

- $\mathcal{F}$ : ensemble of families of ligand

- $f$ : one specific family of ligand

- $\alpha \in [\text{o}, \text{c}]$: index for open/close or leakiness sometimes.

- $K_\alpha^{(r,\ell)}$: dissociation constant of the channel in the $\alpha$ state, between a receptor $r$ and a ligand $\ell$

- $\Delta E_\alpha^{(r,\ell)} = E_\alpha^{(r,\ell)}= E_\text{bound} - E_\text{unbound}$: energy difference between the bound and unbound state.

- $\epsilon_r = E_c - E_o$: energy difference between the closed and opened state of the channel without ligand. Leakiness $=e^\epsilon$

- $\theta = \{(\Delta E_\alpha^{(r,\ell)})_{\alpha,\ell}, \epsilon_r\}_r = \{\theta_r\}_r$ compact indexing for the characteristics of a channel

- $\bar{M} = \{ \mu_\alpha^{(r,f)} \}_{r,f}$ and  $\bar{\Sigma} = \{ \sigma_\alpha^{(r,f)}\}_{r,f}$: respectively the matrix of the mean and std deviation of the distribution from which the energies are drawn for the receptor $r$ in response to the family of ligands $f$. <font color="red"> should the matrices depends on alpha, or should one be a shifted version of the other. In anyway the shift should be the same for every ligands, for a given receptor</font>

- $w$ generic name for $\mu_\alpha^{(r,f)}$ and $\sigma_\alpha^{(r,f)}$.

- $N$: total number of receptors in the array.

- $\mathcal{A} = (a_0,a_1, \cdots , a_N)$: activity value of the array of receptors.

## Vocabulary

- Receptor: a single ion channel.
- Unit: a single protein that constitue a receptor.
- ligand: an arbitrary molecule.
- Pocket: the region of the unit within which a ligand bind.
- Residue: a piece of molecule that composes the pocket or/and a ligand.

## 1. Experimental procedure

Our research investigates the generation of functional diversity in octopus olfactory channels, which are understood to be pentamers ($k_\text{sub}=5$) formed from a pool of $n_\text{unit} = 26$ different proteins (e.g., 'CR518', 'CR918', etc.). We hypothesize that an olfactory cell's response to a ligand is not from a single, defined channel type but rather from a combinatorial ensemble of all possible pentameric stoichiometries and combinatories (e.g., AAAAA, ABBBB, AABBB, ABABB etc..) based on the genes it expresses. 
We want to **measure the diversity of resposne generated by combining hetero-pentamers**

We will fit the experimental dose-response curves for the homopentamers 'CR518' and 'CR918', as well as the heteropentameric 'CR518\_918' combination, across a panel of 11 ligands (including "Medroxyprogesterone", "Androsterone", "Progesterone", etc.).
-> Experimental dose-response curves measure the current generated by the diffusion of small ions through a membrane.
-> This current is proportional to the opening probability of the channels embedded in the membrane.
-> To rule-out the influence of this proportionality factor, we focus our fitting procedure on the renormalize current:
$$
\text{renormalized current} = \frac{I(c) - I_\text{min}}{I_\text{max}-I_\text{min}} = \frac{p_\text{open}(c) - p_\text{min}}{p_\text{max}-p_\text{min}}
